Most small business websites look fine on the surface. Clean layout, a logo, some text about services. But they're invisible on Google, get no enquiries, and quietly fail the business they were meant to help.

Here's why — and what actually works.

## 1. No clear target keyword strategy

Your site can't rank for "everything." If you sell accountancy services in Manchester, your homepage needs to say that clearly and repeatedly. Google needs to understand exactly what you do and who you serve before it shows you to anyone.

**Fix:** Identify 3–5 core keywords your customers actually search. Build a page around each one.

## 2. Slow load times

A site that takes more than 3 seconds to load loses over half its visitors before they've seen a single word. Most small business sites are bloated with unoptimised images, outdated plugins, or cheap shared hosting.

**Fix:** Compress images, use a fast host, and run your site through Google PageSpeed Insights.

## 3. No local SEO signals

If you serve a local area, your site needs to tell Google that. That means your city name in page titles, a Google Business Profile that matches your site details, and local backlinks.

**Fix:** Set up and fully complete your Google Business Profile. Add your location to key pages.

## 4. Calls to action that don't convert

"Contact us" is not a call to action. It's a door with no handle. Visitors need to be told what to do next, why now, and what they'll get.

**Fix:** Use specific CTAs: "Get a free quote in 24 hours" beats "Contact us" every time.
