Most businesses approach social media like a treadmill — the moment you stop, everything stops. That's exhausting, and it's not how strong brands are built.

Here's a more sustainable approach.

## Quality over frequency

One well-thought-out post per week will outperform seven rushed ones. Audiences follow accounts that make them feel something — informed, entertained, understood. That takes intention, not just volume.

Start with one platform. Do it well. Expand only when you have a rhythm.

## Batch your content

The biggest time drain in social media is context-switching — stopping what you're doing to think of something to post. Instead, set aside two hours once a month to plan and create a full month of content.

Use a simple spreadsheet: date, platform, format, caption, visual. Prepare everything in one session. Schedule it. Done.

## Repurpose ruthlessly

One blog post can become:

- A carousel breaking down the key points
- Three quote graphics
- A short-form video summary
- A LinkedIn article
- A thread

You're not creating more content — you're getting more life out of what you've already made.

## Measure what matters

Vanity metrics (likes, follower count) feel good but don't pay bills. Focus on:

- **Profile visits** — are people curious enough to check you out?
- **Link clicks** — are they going to your site?
- **DMs and enquiries** — are they reaching out?

If none of those are moving, the content isn't working — regardless of how many likes it gets.

## When to consider outsourcing

If social media is consistently the thing you feel guilty about not doing, it's time to outsource it. The cost of a managed social presence is almost always less than the mental load of doing it badly yourself.
