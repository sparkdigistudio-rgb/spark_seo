The range of website prices in 2026 is enormous — from £0 (DIY on Wix) to £50,000+ for a custom enterprise build. Most small businesses sit somewhere in the middle, often confused about what they're paying for and why prices vary so wildly.

Let's break it down honestly.

## The real cost of "free" website builders

Wix, Squarespace, and similar platforms are not actually free. The free tiers come with the platform's branding, limited pages, and no custom domain. Paid plans start around £10–20/month, which adds up to £120–240/year — indefinitely.

More importantly: they're built for ease, not performance. They tend to be slower, harder to optimise for SEO, and difficult to migrate away from later.

## Freelancer (£300–£1,500)

A freelance developer or designer can build a solid brochure site for a small business in this range. Quality varies significantly. At the lower end, you're often getting a theme install with your content dropped in. At the higher end, you can get genuinely custom design and SEO-ready structure.

**Watch out for:** No ongoing support, no SEO, no strategy — just a website that sits there.

## Agency (£1,500–£10,000+)

Agencies bring more process, more people, and more accountability. A mid-range agency project in this bracket should include design, development, copywriting, basic SEO setup, and a handover process.

**What you're paying for:** Strategy, not just execution.

## What actually determines ROI

The price of the site matters far less than what it does after it's live. A £500 site that ranks for the right keywords and converts visitors into enquiries is worth more than a £5,000 site that looks beautiful and does nothing.

The question to ask isn't "how much does a website cost?" — it's "what will this website make me?"
