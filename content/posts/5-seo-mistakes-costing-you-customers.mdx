SEO mistakes don't always look like mistakes. Sometimes your site looks perfectly fine — it's just not ranking, not getting clicks, and not converting. Here are five of the most common culprits.

## 1. Duplicate page titles

Every page on your site should have a unique title tag. When multiple pages share the same title, Google gets confused about which one to show — so it often shows neither.

**Fix:** Audit your page titles. Every single one should be unique and include a target keyword.

## 2. Images with no alt text

Search engines can't see images — they read the alt text instead. Skipping alt text means missed keyword opportunities and a worse experience for screen reader users.

**Fix:** Add descriptive alt text to every image. Be specific: "Manchester SEO agency team meeting" beats "image1.jpg".

## 3. No internal linking

If your blog posts and service pages aren't linking to each other, you're losing authority and making it harder for Google to crawl your site.

**Fix:** Every blog post should link to at least one relevant service page. Every service page should link to supporting content.

## 4. Targeting keywords nobody searches

"Best premium digital solutions provider" — nobody types that into Google. Keyword research isn't optional; it's the foundation everything else is built on.

**Fix:** Use Google's autocomplete, or a free tool like Ubersuggest, to find what your customers actually search for.

## 5. Not tracking anything

If you can't see what's working, you can't improve it. Many small business sites have no analytics set up at all.

**Fix:** Install Google Analytics 4 and Google Search Console. Both are free. Both are essential.
